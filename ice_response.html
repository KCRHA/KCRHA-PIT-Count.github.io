CRITICAL: Under the Keep Washington Working Act and King County policy, you are protected when refusing to assist with federal civil immigration enforcement.

1. Immediate Response

  • Stay Calm and Do Not Run: Movement can be used as a pretext for detention. Stand your ground calmly.
  • Record the Interaction: Take out your phone and begin filming. Document badge numbers, names, and vehicle plates.
  • Maintain Silence: You are not required to provide information about yourself or the individuals you are surveying. State: "I am exercising my right to remain silent."

2. Legal Boundaries & Entry

  • Demand a Judicial Warrant: Do not allow entry to any private facility, vehicle, or designated counting area without a warrant signed by a Judge.
  • Reject Administrative Warrants: Forms I-200 or I-205 (signed only by ICE) do not grant legal authority to enter private premises without consent.

3. Reporting Procedures

  • Alert Site Lead Immediately: Notify your supervisor as soon as it is safe to do so.
  • Contact WAISN: Report the activity to the Washington Immigrant Solidarity Network.
